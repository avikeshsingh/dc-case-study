Conversion Optimisation

5 changes that will directly increase bookings

01

Sticky Mobile Booking Button

+20–35% mobile conversion

Fixed footer bar that follows the user as they scroll. On mobile — where 80%+ of traffic comes from — this is the single highest-impact change possible.

02

Hero Social Proof

+15–25% trust uplift

Star rating and review count immediately below the hero headline. Anchors trust before visitors have read a single word of copy.

03

Real Haircut Gallery

+20% booking conversion

Visitors book what they see. The gallery should be the second section — not buried. Real photos of Kristian's work outperform any copywriting.

04

CTA After Gallery

Captures impulse booking

"Get This Cut" placed immediately after the gallery — at the exact moment visitors are most inspired by the work they've just seen.

05

Pricing Upfront

Removes friction, builds trust

Transparent pricing removes the #1 objection before booking. Clients who see pricing upfront convert at higher rates than those who have to ask.

CTA Map Rule

Place 5 booking CTAs throughout the page. Visitors book at different moments of trust — never make them search for a button.

Hero→ Services→ Gallery→ Reviews→ Footer

10

Local SEO Strategy

Starting from zero — here's the roadmap to Google visibility

⚡

Current State: Zero Web Presence

Square's booking page has no SEO value. There are no indexable pages, no metadata, no schema markup, and no local search signals. The opportunity here is total — every improvement is a gain from a blank slate. In Hillside, the local SEO competition is weak. This is a window.

Priority SEO Actions

01

Google Business Profile

Highest Impact

Claim and fully optimise the Google Business Profile. Set correct name, address (Hillside VIC 3037), hours, category (Barber Shop), photos, and booking link. This drives Google Maps rankings directly — the most important single action.

02

Local Schema Markup

High Impact

Implement LocalBusiness + BarberShop schema on the homepage. Include NAP (Name, Address, Phone), opening hours, and geo coordinates. Tells Google exactly what the business is and where it is.

03

Keyword Targeting

High Impact

Target hyper-local keywords: "barber Hillside VIC", "barber Caroline Springs", "barber Keilor", "best barber Melbourne west". Include suburb names naturally in title tags, H1s, and the first paragraph of homepage copy.

04

On-Page Foundations

Medium Impact

Unique title tag: "Directors Cut Hair Studio | Barber Hillside VIC". Meta description with CTA and location. One clear H1 per page. Descriptive image alt text. Consistent NAP across every page on the site.

05

Review Strategy

High Impact

Google reviews are a direct local ranking signal. Ask top regulars to leave a Google review after each great cut. Aim for 25+ reviews in the first 3 months. Respond to every review — Google rewards engagement.

06

Local Content

Medium Impact

Blog posts targeting Hillside and Melbourne west searches. Each post builds topical authority and creates new pages for Google to index. Title format: "[Service] in [Suburb] — [benefit]".

SEO Advantage

"A well-optimised website with a claimed Google Business Profile can rank on page 1 for 'barber Hillside' within 60–90 days. The local SEO competition in Hillside is weak — the window to own the local search result is open right now."

Referral & Word-of-Mouth

Kristian's most powerful growth channel — formalised

→

Why This Matters

Kristian is already well known in Melbourne and recognised across parts of Australia — word-of-mouth is already working. The goal is to give it structure so it scales. Referred clients have higher loyalty, lower churn, and refer others at 2–3x the rate of non-referred clients.

Referral Mechanics That Work for a Barbershop

Start Here

Bring-a-Friend Card

Give every regular a physical card: "Bring a mate and you both get $10 off your next cut." Double-sided rewards — both parties win. Low friction, high conversion. Works offline, no tech setup required.

Referrer: $10 off ↔ New client: $10 off

Loyalty Stamp Card

10 cuts → 1 free. Simple, proven, and it gets clients coming back. Physical cards feel tangible and personal — a barbershop staple that still outperforms digital alternatives.

School & JRL Partner Discount

First-cut deal for students from partner schools. Builds the next generation of regulars and reinforces the community narrative that already makes Kristian stand out from every other barber in Hillside.

Instagram Tag Referral

Clients tag the shop + their "who to see" recommendation → gets them a discount on next visit. Combines referral with organic social proof. Low effort for the client, high reach for the brand.

Client of the Month

Shout out a regular on Instagram. Tag them, tell their story. Regulars feel genuinely valued — and new clients see real people who love the shop, not just a barbershop's marketing page.

The Post-Cut Ask

After a great cut, ask: "Know anyone looking for a barber?" Most barbers never ask — the ones that do get the bulk of their new clients this way. It's the simplest referral mechanic of all.
